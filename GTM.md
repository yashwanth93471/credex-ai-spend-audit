# Go-to-market

> Day 1 placeholder — fleshed out across Days 4–5 once the product is stable
> enough to demo to a potential customer. Below is the working hypothesis.

## ICP (ideal customer profile)

Engineering-led startups, 5–50 employees, that have accumulated 3+ AI
subscriptions across the team without a procurement function. Combined AI
spend $300–$3,000/mo. Decision-maker: founding engineer, CTO, or
ops-of-one. Cares about cost discipline but won't build a spreadsheet.

## Why now

- AI subscription sprawl is real and visible on credit-card statements
- Most tools list-publish plans, so an audit can be done from declared inputs
- LLM-generated narrative makes a $1.80-cost-of-goods report feel bespoke

## Acquisition channels (ranked by likely CAC)

1. **Founder-network share-link virality** — every audit produces a
   shareable URL with a "saved $X/mo" OG image. Free distribution.
2. **Twitter / LinkedIn organic** — post sample audits with redacted inputs.
3. **Indie Hackers / Y Combinator forums** — niche audience, high intent.
4. **Cold email to YC/Techstars batches** — scoped, personalised.

## Conversion funnel

```
Land  →  Start form  →  Submit  →  See report  →  Capture email  →  Reply / book call
```

Target conversion rates (Day 1 hypothesis, not measured):

| Step              | Target |
| ----------------- | -----: |
| Land → start form | 25%    |
| Start → submit    | 60%    |
| Submit → email    | 35%    |
| Email → reply     | 10%    |

## Positioning

> "Most teams overpay for AI tools by 30%. Credex finds it in 60 seconds."

The promise: a useful artifact (the report), not a product to learn.
